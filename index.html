About Me

I am a T32 Postdoctoral Scholar in Behavioral Geriatrics at Weill Cornell Medicine's Division of Geriatrics and Palliative Medicine. Trained as a public health physician, my interdisciplinary scholarship lies at the nexus of health policy and health system research, health service research, and health longevity.

My current research aims at two interrelated areas, focusing on both the United States and China — the largest developed country and the largest developing country. First, I use human-centered AI approaches and consumer digital health informatics to support decision-making for health management activities among aging adults. Second, I seek to understand how these technologies reshape their social surroundings, including the meaning of care, community moral economies, and the dynamics between patients, providers, and the state.

I earned my PhD in Family Medicine and Primary Care from McGill University, an MSc in Health Policy and Management from Peking University, and my MBBS from Fudan University. I also hold a bachelor's degree in economics from Peking University. My research has been supported by the FRQS Doctoral Training Award, China Scholarship Council Fellowship, McGill Global Health Scholars Program, Brocher Foundation Junior Researcher Scholarship, and the AGE-WELL Travel Bursary, among others.
